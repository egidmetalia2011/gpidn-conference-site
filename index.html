Global Prisons Infectious Diseases Network

The Global Prisons Infectious Diseases Network connects researchers, healthcare professionals, policymakers, and advocates committed to preventing and treating the most prevalent infectious diseases in custodial settings (prisons, jails, community corrections, and other closed settings), including hepatitis B (HBV), hepatitis C (HCV), HIV, tuberculosis (TB), and sexually transmitted infections (STIs).

Knowledge Exchange

Facilitating global knowledge sharing and best practices in prison healthcare through workshops, conferences, and webinars. Our events bring together experts worldwide to share insights and innovations in prison research and healthcare delivery.

Recent Highlights

  • Annual conferences connecting global experts
  • Annual interactive bibliography on cutting edge research
  • Webinars on pressing carceral healthcare issues

Research

Advancing evidence-based understanding of infectious diseases in prison settings through collaborative research initiatives and knowledge synthesis.

Recent Highlights

  • Evidence-based practice guidelines
  • ‍Data-driven policy recommendations‍
  • Advancing hepatitis C care in low- and middle-income country prisons

Advocacy

Championing policy change and improved healthcare access in correctional facilities through strategic advocacy and stakeholder engagement.

Recent Highlights

  • Assessment of barriers and advocacy needs for hepatitis C services in prisons
  • Co-designing a Prisons Hepatitis C Advocacy Toolkit
  • Hepatitis C Advocacy Toolkit to strengthen prison-based hepatitis C services
